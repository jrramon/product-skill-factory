# Business Strategy — The Strategy Kernel

Apply the Strategy Kernel framework from "Good Strategy Bad Strategy" by Richard Rumelt. Strategy is scarcity's child — it demands the will to say "No" to interests and actions that scatter resources.

## The Kernel — Three Elements in Order

### 1. Diagnosis
Defines the nature of the challenge. Simplifies complexity by identifying critical aspects.
- Must not confuse the challenge with underperformance. Underperformance is an outcome; the challenge is the reason behind it
- Seek simplicity and focus — replace overwhelming complexity with a simple story highlighting crucial aspects
- Identify the "elephant in the elevator" — the critical obstacle everyone avoids naming

### 2. Guiding Policy
The general approach chosen to overcome obstacles identified in the Diagnosis.
- Defines a method for addressing the situation
- Channels and constrains action — excludes broad ranges of possible moves
- Creates or uses leverage or advantage
- Must not be confused with goals or visions
- Must exploit asymmetries and work around organizational constraints

### 3. Coherent Actions
Coordinated steps to carry out the Guiding Policy, anchored in Proximate Objectives.
- Actions must be consistent and mutually reinforcing — no conflict or resource dilution
- Proximate Objectives must be feasible — close enough for the organization to achieve
- If the objective is as hard as the original challenge, it's a "Blue Sky Objective" and adds no strategic value
- Leadership must absorb complexity and hand the organization a solvable problem

## Coaching Process

Guide through the three steps one at a time. Do not allow progression without rigorously validating the current step.

**Step 1** — "What is the primary challenge? Provide an explicit description connecting facts into patterns and surfacing critical issues."

**Step 2** — "Based on the Diagnosis, what is your Guiding Policy? What method, what constraints, what source of leverage?"

**Step 3** — "What Coherent Actions and Proximate Objectives will implement the policy? What specific, coordinated steps concentrate energy and resources?"

## Hallmarks of Bad Strategy — Challenge Immediately

- **Fluff** — abstract jargon masking absence of strategic thinking
- **Failure to face the challenge** — no diagnosis of critical obstacles
- **Goals for strategy** — ambitious targets or visions presented as strategy
- **Dog's Dinner objectives** — messy list scattering energy without focus
- **Blue Sky objectives** — restating desired state, ignoring difficulty of getting there

## Key Concepts

- **Leverage** — concentrate force at a pivot that magnifies effect, exploiting rival asymmetries
- **Chain-Link Systems** — performance constrained by weakest link; fix weak links sequentially
- **Inertia and Entropy** — rivals' inability to adapt is an opportunity; organizations naturally become disorganized without active maintenance
- **Dynamic Advantage** — increase value of existing competitive advantage through continual innovation
- **Coordination vs Resources** — fewer resources demand smarter, tighter integration
