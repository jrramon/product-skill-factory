# Impact-Driven Growth

Move from a feature factory (outputs) to behavioral changes (outcomes) that drive real business impact.

## Core Philosophy

1. **Impact Backwards** — define desired impact first, then identify necessary behavioral changes, then design experiments
2. **Antidote to Delusion** — use data and measurable behavior as proof, not vanity metrics or intentions
3. **Learn Before Executing** — validate highest risks through small, iterative experiments

## Phase 1: Context Gathering

Gather context with these four specific questions:

1. **Describe the challenge** — current situation and specific friction points
2. **The 8-12 week goal** — if you could only improve one specific thing in 8-12 weeks, what would it be?
3. **Problem category** — inefficiency, customer complaints, high risk/compliance, or slow growth?
4. **The success paragraph** — describe in one paragraph what would have to happen for you to say "this is working"

## Phase 2: Architecture of Impact

Build the impact hierarchy:

- **CPVM (Customer Perceived Value Metric)** — measures concrete value delivered to the customer within the specific challenge. Unlike a North Star metric, the CPVM is non-permanent and actionable within weeks, not years
- **Impact Metrics (Leading Metrics)** — 1-3 metrics that serve as short-term guides. Must be leading (predictive) not lagging (historical)
- **Outcomes** — behavioral changes using the formula: **"Who does what by how much?"**. Must be observable changes in human actions

## Phase 3: Experimentation Plan

- **Initiatives (Outputs)** — specific actions designed to trigger the identified outcomes
- **Hypothesis Formulation** — create a triad for each: "If we implement **[Initiative]**, then **[Persona]** will **[Outcome]**, which will impact **[Impact Metric]**"
- **Detailed Experiments** — for each hypothesis:
  - User Benefits: why would the user care?
  - Learnings: what specific risk are we validating?
  - Success Criteria: what is the fail/pass threshold?
  - Risk Level: low, medium, or high based on team constraints

## Key Concepts

- **OMTM** — every stage (Empathy, Stickiness, Virality, Revenue, Scale) requires a different "One Metric That Matters"
- **Leading vs Lagging** — a leading metric (content consumption) predicts the lagging metric (sales conversion)
- **Outcomes vs Benefits** — an outcome is a measurable change in behavior; a benefit is the positive result of that change

## Anti-patterns to challenge

- Suggesting vanity metrics (page views, total sign-ups) — steer toward actionable/leading metrics
- Being too broad — help verticalize the challenge into a manageable niche
- Jumping to solutions without defining outcomes first
- Confusing outputs (features shipped) with outcomes (behavior changed)

Always explain the chain of impact from tactical experiment up to the CPVM.
