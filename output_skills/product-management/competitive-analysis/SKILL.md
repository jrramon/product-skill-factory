# Competitive Analysis

Analyze competitors to inform product strategy and positioning.

## Process

### 1. Define scope
- Which competitors to analyze (or suggest based on market)
- Scope: feature comparison, pricing, full analysis, or specific angle
- What decision will this analysis inform?

### 2. Competitor profiling
For each competitor:
- What they do (positioning, value proposition)
- Target audience and segments
- Key differentiators
- Pricing model and tiers
- Market presence and traction signals

### 3. Feature comparison
- Build a comparison matrix: your product vs competitors
- Identify feature parity, gaps, and unique advantages
- Note quality differences — having a feature vs having it well

### 4. SWOT per competitor
- **Strengths** — what they do better
- **Weaknesses** — where they fall short
- **Opportunities** — gaps you can exploit
- **Threats** — risks they pose to your position

### 5. Strategic implications
- Where are you differentiated vs commoditized?
- What are the must-have table stakes?
- Where can you create unique advantage?
- What positioning strategy makes sense?

## Output Structure

1. **Overview** — competitive landscape summary
2. **Competitor Profiles** — one section per competitor
3. **Feature Comparison Matrix** — your product vs each competitor
4. **Pricing Analysis** — model comparison
5. **SWOT Summary** — per competitor
6. **Strategic Implications** — differentiation and positioning
7. **Opportunities** — gaps to exploit
8. **Threats** — risks to mitigate
9. **Recommended Actions** — prioritized strategic moves

## Anti-patterns to avoid

- Feature-counting without assessing quality and user experience
- Ignoring indirect competitors and substitutes
- Confirmation bias — looking for reasons your product is better
- Stale analysis — competitive landscapes change fast
- Analyzing without connecting to a specific decision or strategy
