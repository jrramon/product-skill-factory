# Roadmap Planning

Build a strategic, prioritized roadmap that communicates intent, not just features.

## Process

### 1. Define scope
- Timeframe (quarter, half, year)
- New roadmap or update to existing
- Audience (team, stakeholders, executives — determines detail level)

### 2. Strategic themes
- Define 3-5 strategic themes that connect to business goals
- Each theme should answer: "Why are we investing here?"
- Themes are the "why", features are the "what"

### 3. Gather inputs
- User needs and pain points (from research/feedback)
- Business goals and OKRs
- Competitive context and market timing
- Technical debt and platform needs
- Stakeholder requests and commitments

### 4. Prioritize and sequence
- Apply prioritization framework (RICE, Value vs Effort, etc.)
- Consider dependencies between features
- Account for team capacity and availability
- Balance: new features vs improvements vs debt vs research

### 5. Structure the roadmap
- Organize by time period (month/quarter)
- Group features under strategic themes
- Show dependencies between items
- Mark confidence levels: committed / planned / exploratory

## Output Structure

1. **Strategic Context** — business goals this roadmap serves
2. **Themes** — 3-5 themes with rationale
3. **Roadmap by Period** — features grouped by theme and time
4. **Dependencies** — what blocks what
5. **Capacity & Effort** — rough sizing per period
6. **Success Criteria** — how we know the roadmap is working
7. **Risks & Assumptions** — what could change this plan
8. **Open Questions** — decisions still needed

## Roadmap communication tips

- Lead with outcomes, not features — "Improve onboarding conversion" not "Build wizard"
- Use confidence levels — don't present everything as committed
- Show trade-offs — what you chose NOT to do and why
- Keep it alive — roadmaps are living documents, not contracts

## Anti-patterns to avoid

- Feature lists without strategic context — a roadmap is not a backlog
- Committing to exact dates for exploratory work
- Overloading the roadmap — if everything is a priority, nothing is
- No room for learning — leave space for discoveries and pivots
- Building the roadmap alone — roadmaps need cross-functional input
