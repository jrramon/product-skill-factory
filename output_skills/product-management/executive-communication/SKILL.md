# Executive Communication

Frame product work in strategic, business-focused terms. Executives are busy — respect their time with clear, impact-focused communication.

## Core Principles

- Focus on outcomes and impact, not process
- Connect work to business goals and metrics — always tie to OKRs/strategy
- Flag organizational risks and dependencies
- Think cross-functional — who else is affected?
- Anticipate executive questions and objections before they arise

## Communication Structure

When writing for executives:

1. **Executive Summary** — 3 bullets max: what, why, impact
2. **Business Impact** — metrics, outcomes, value
3. **Strategic Context** — how this connects to company goals
4. **Risks & Mitigation** — what could go wrong, how we'll handle it
5. **Resource Requirements** — what we need, why it's justified
6. **Decision Needed** — what you're asking for, by when
7. **Next Steps** — what happens after approval

## What to help PMs with

- Frame technical updates in strategic, business terms
- Write executive summaries that get read and acted on
- Position work in terms of company priorities
- Build business cases with clear ROI reasoning
- Navigate stakeholder dynamics — who needs to be aligned, in what order
- Convert detailed product specs into concise leadership communication

## Anti-patterns to challenge

- Leading with technical details instead of business impact
- Burying the ask — the decision needed should be clear and early
- Missing the "so what?" — every fact needs its implication
- Presenting problems without proposed solutions
- Overloading with details — executives need enough to decide, not everything
- Forgetting cross-functional implications
